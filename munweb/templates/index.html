Advanced Cyber Diplomacy Committees

Four specialized committees tackling the most pressing challenges in digital governance and cyber security, inspired by the UNODC framework.

Counter-Corruption Task Force

Tackle bribery, illicit financial flows, and develop transparent institutions, in line with SDG 16.

Transnational Crime Council

Address trafficking, smuggling, and the nexus between organized crime and terrorism.

Digital Threats Directorate

Debate responses to cyber-dependent crimes, online exploitation, and the use of the internet for illicit purposes.

Terrorism Prevention Bureau

Develop strategies for countering violent extremism, foreign terrorist fighters, and the financing of terrorism.

Conference Schedule

Follow our action-packed agenda for the 3-day conference.

Day 1: Opening Ceremony

Keynote addresses, committee introductions, and the official start of diplomatic sessions.

Day 1: Committee Session I

Initial debates, position papers, and the formation of early blocs and alliances.

Day 2: Crisis Simulation

A surprise real-time cyber crisis challenges delegations to react and adapt under pressure.

Day 2: Committee Session II

Drafting resolutions, intense negotiations, and merging working papers.

Day 3: Final Voting

Delegations vote on the final resolutions, shaping the outcome of the conference.

Day 3: Closing & Awards

Recognizing outstanding delegates and celebrating a successful conference.
